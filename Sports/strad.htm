The Strad Page

 

History

2004 (Atlanta) - Despite the challenge of playing in a bar with no pitchers, and the constant interruption of needing to refill our pints, Tom makes short work of Mike at Churchill Arms to take a 1-0 lead.

2005 (Houston) - At The Tavern, surrounded by the loudest, most annoying music in the history of strad, Mike evens up the series at 1 game all.

2006 (Houston) - At Pub Fiction, Mike recorded one of the greatest comebacks in all of sports, rallying from down 3-1 and down 200+ points in one game, to take the series lead 2-1.

2007 (Houston) - At Pub Fiction for the 2nd straight year, Mike dominated, playing a good match with just the right amount of luck mixed in for a 4-2 win. 

2008 (Atlanta, played in 2009) - At The Independent, after trying 4 other bars previously, Mike dominated again in the first ever shutout 4-0.

2009 (Houston, played in 2010) - Mike's wedding weekend at Pub Fiction. Mike sweeps 4-0 to earn back to back shutouts.  Mike leads the series 5-1.

2010 - (Knoxville) - At Union Jack's, the seemingly only bar in TN with a real dart board, Mike rallied down from 3-1 (again) to take a 6-1 lead in the series.

2011 - (Houston, played in 2013) - At Dog House Tavern, with their skunky beer, after 2 Perfect Margaritas each, Tom dominated 4-1 ending a 6 match losing streak.  Mike leads the series 6-2.

2012 - in progress, started at Dog House Tavern after the 2011 match, Tom leads 1-0.  Play was suspended when we were kicked out of the bar at closing time.

Rules

1)     Standard cricket rules apply unless otherwise stated in these rules.

2)     If a player marks with all 3 darts in a turn, his opponent has to plug (1 plug = max of 3 sips). 3 doubles in a turn = double plug.  Any Round 7 or better = finish.  If a player scores points on all 3 darts while in the lead or in process of taking the lead, he must sip along with the opponent's plug.

3)     If a player does not mark with any of his 3 darts in a turn, he takes 1 sip * the number of consecutive turns in which he has not marked.  For example, missing all three darts the first time = 1 sip.  Missing all 3 a second consecutive time = 2 sips.  This progression continues until a) the player marks b) the player finishes (in this case he has to finish on each consecutive turn until he marks) c) a timeout is called d) the game is forfeited.

4)     Each player gets 1 timeout to start the game and an additional timeout at the start of every odd numbered game.

5)     Offs:  An off is defined as a dart that does not hit the board inside of the double points circle (inclusive).  Each off will be recorded on the Off line.  A player can score an unlimited amount of Offs.  The only thing that can remove an off from a player's score is a 1.  Three offs in one turn incurs a finish penalty.

6)     Ones: A one is defined as a dart that lands in the 1 position of the board.  Singles, doubles, and triples count as usual.  Each One will be recorded on the One line.  A player can score an unlimited amount of ones.  The only thing that can remove a one from a player's score is an off.

7)     At the end of the game, after a player of a standard cricket game would be declared a winner, one point is added to each player's score for each One that he has recorded.  At this same time, five points are subtracted from each player's score for each Off that he has recorded.  Final scores are calculated, the winner is the player with the most points, regardless of who's board is closed.

8)     If a player hits a 1 and a 19 on any 2 of his 3 darts, he has scored a "One-Nine".  He marks his 1 (or erases an off if he has offs), marks his 19 (if still open), and marks his other dart if it scored.  If the other dart is an Off, the player plugs and the turn is then over.  If it is not an Off and is inside the triples circle, the player's opponent sips, and the player re-throws the non one-nine dart.  If he marks, he scores the mark, the opponent sips, and his turn is over.  If he doesn't mark, but the dart lands inside the triple circle (non-inclusive), the opponent sips and he re-throws the dart as if it were his first re-throw.  If he doesn't mark, or the dart lands outside the triple circle (inclusive), the player's turn is over.  Note that if any re-throw results in an Off, the player marks the off and plugs.  Then his turn is over.

9)     If a player hits a 2 and a 3 on any 2 of his 3 darts, he has scored a "Two-Three".  He marks his other dart if it has scored.  If the other dart is an Off, the player plugs.  If it is not an Off, but it is outside the triples circle (inclusive), the player sips.  If it is inside the triples circle (not-inclusive), the opponent sips.  There are no re-throws on a Two-Three.

10)  Dropping the chalk costs you one sip per fragment of chalk on the ground that resulted from the drop unless you can catch it before it hits the ground.  If you drop the chalk and your opponent catches it before it hits the ground, you must buy and drink a shot of your opponent's choice (have no mercy on the shot selection...  i.e. Wild Turkey is in play).

11)  If at any time a player hits a 16 and a 19 with any 2 of his 3 darts, the round is declared a social and both players socially sip.

12)  Each player has 10 seconds between each dart throw, and 30 seconds between turns.  Violating the time limit costs the player a dart for every 10 seconds of violation until the turn is over.

13)  Timeouts last 60 seconds.  Following a timeout, a player still gets 10 seconds if the timeout was taken in the middle of a turn (after throwing the first dart) or 30 seconds if it was at the start/end of a turn.  Timeouts may be called at any time that a dart is not in flight.

14)  Any dart landing in a sign for an alcoholic beverage of any kind means that the thrower of the dart has to buy a round of that beverage.

15)  Loser of each game buys the next pitcher.  Loser of the match buys shots.

16)  A player may forfeit a game at any time by finishing his beer and declaring the forfeit.

17) The series is a best of 7 games.

18)  Closest to the cork goes first in the first game.  Each additional game, alternates starting players.

Etiquette (Note: as with any drinking game, there is disagreement - of course - with the proper rules of etiquette)

1) No talking in your opponent's backswing.

2) Never let your opponent's beer go empty.

3) It is unsportsmanlike to shoot for points when in the lead, unless your opponent has scored bulls already.

4) Keep the celebration to a minimum when your opponent is struggling.

5) Refrain from calling "buffalo".  In fact, suspend all other drinking games (except false ac).

6) Drink for stupidity, wrongness, and especially insisting.

7) Don't buy Bud Light ever. 

8) Rule 7 may be broken if Miller Genuine Headache (MGD) is the only other choice.

9) All game interruptions are unacceptable unless said interruptions are extremely hot.
